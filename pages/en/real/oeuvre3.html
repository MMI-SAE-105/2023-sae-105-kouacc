Centre de Culture et des Congrès (KKL)

Lucerne, Switzerland

Details

  • Status: completed
  • Completion date: 1993 - 2000
  • Floor area: 22 000m² / 35 000m²
  • Mission type: competition

Lucerne is located at the end of a lake: the two parts of the city face each other and look out to the mountains beyond. The surrounding landscape, summer and winter alike, provides a rich backdrop to the city's historically varied existing architecture and dense urban fabric. A new building therefore had to begin with what Nouvel calls a "principle of inclusion", in which the lake itself is the major element. If the building can't reach the shoreline, then the lake will come to the building. The backbone of the complex is thus placed far from the lake, while the three main elements (the two concert halls, the conference center) extend from this spine towards the lake, separated from each other at ground-floor level by lines of water, "an aquatic garden" according to Nouvel, crossed by footbridges. The museum is located above the west building, the conference center. These elements are united by an immense sloping copper roof, which projects, unsupported, twenty meters into the air. Jean Nouvel
