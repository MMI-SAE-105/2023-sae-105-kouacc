Jean Nouvel

Jean Nouvel was born in Fumel (France) in 1945.

After studying at the École des Beaux-Arts in Bordeaux, he came first in the entrance examination for the École Nationale Supérieure des Beaux-Arts in Paris in 1966, graduating in 1971. Initially an assistant to architect Claude Parent, inspired by urbanist and essayist Paul Virilio, he opened his first agency in 1970. He later co-founded the "Mars 1976" movement to combat the corporatism of architects, and later the Syndicat de l'architecture.

His committed stance on the integration of architecture into the urban context and the constantly renewed originality of his projects around the world have helped forge his international image. His approach, which eschews stylistic considerations, is guided solely by the moment, the site, its history and its environment.
