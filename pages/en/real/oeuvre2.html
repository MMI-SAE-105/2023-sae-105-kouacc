Détails

  • Status: completed
  • Completion date: 1991 - 1994
  • Floor area 22 000m² / 35 000m²
  • Owner: GAN VIE

The ghost of the park. In its transparency. Inclusion. The trees can be seen behind the high glass fence that has taken the place of the long blind wall. Embedded in an eight-metre-high wall, they gently brush against it. The Chateaubriand cedar stands alone, framed by two screens that assert the entrance. Visitors pass beneath the cedar and are treated to the spectacle of the trees surrounding the eight-metre-high glass exhibition room, giving an in-depth reading of the site. In summer, the large sliding windows disappear and the room becomes an extension of the park, punctuated by high stilts. The architecture is all lightness, glazing and finely woven steel. An architecture whose game consists in blurring the tangible limits of the building and rendering the reading of a solid volume superfluous, in a poetics of vagueness and evanescence. When virtuality attacks reality, architecture, more than ever, must have the courage to assume the image of contradiction. Jean Nouvel
